About Me

High School senior

Hello, I'm Daniel Tadesse

I am a senior at Mount Rainier High School with an interest in Computer Science. I was born in Jimma, Ethiopia, and moved to America when I was 6. Despite battling a language barrier, I love meeting new people and making new friends! Last summer I was a part of the first cohort in the UW Changemakers in Computing internship program, where I found my passion for computer science and using technology for real-life implementation. I am currently pursuing my AA Degree in Computer Science at Highline Community College Through Dual Enrollment. Eventually, I plan on attending a university and getting my Bachelor's or Masters's degree.

My Hobbies

Basketball

I play varsity basketball for my school as well as for AAU clubs. I have been playing basketball since 7th grade and it's my favorite hobby. Through basketball, I've learned hard work and dedication. Basketball is a common language when meeting new people and has opened many new doors for me.

Black Student Union

I am President of my schools Black Student Union. I meet with other officers weekly to play meetings, organize events, and set up fundraisers. We have had guest speakers such as Senayet Negusse of the Seatac City Council, as well as organizations like Student Connection. This year we have set a new MRHS BSU record of over 60 members!

Young Executives Of Color

Selected into a 9-month Program at the University of Washington Foster School of Business. I spend Saturdays at YEOC to work on professional development, marketing, and case competitions. Through YEOC, I've learned important life skills like self-branding, collaboration, and networking.
